Loadout

As a vaguely technical online person, it is my duty to force upon you a mediocre list of hardware without ever being prompted.

MacBook Air M1

Poster child of the Apple Silicon transition and OBJECTIVELY THE IDEAL COMPUTER. With 16GB of memory it just keeps purring along.

It spends half of its life as a real, laptopy laptop. The other half of the time, it tends to be accompanied by:

Peripherals

Okay so here's the thing: I've been min-maxing my electronics for as long as I remember. Trying to find the nicest product for the absolute least amount of money, often spending ridiculous hours of research doing so, is something that has just always made sense for me. And most importantly: it's fun.

So the stuff listed below tends to have a common trait: being dirt-cheap while offering more than meets the eye. Let's take a look!

Dell S2240L

Hear me out, alright? So first, this thing has an IPS panel, which is not quite standard in old budget monitors, as far as I can tell. Second, there's a ✨glossy✨ finish, which is also surprisingly rare! Both of these have the combined effect of producing pretty decent colors.

(Ever noticed what all of Apple's displays have in common? Yeah that's right, they're usually (1) IPS, and (2) glossy!)

Then there's the resolution: while yes it's only 1080p, it's also in a pretty small 21.5" package. This makes the display around 100 DPI – that actually works just right for non-retina viewing… Which is becoming less of a relevant thing each day, however I'll still take it over a non-integer blurry mess in the DPI “bad zone” (around 160). And on the rare occasion that a developer still cares about pixel-perfect graphics and goes the extra mile to add some crisp icons, it's pretty glorious!

Logitech K750

One of the very few cheap, full-size Mac keyboards. I actually got it for 25€ from a wonderful person on eBay. It has this bizarre solar-charging function I've never seen anywhere else, but hey, it seems to work so far! It's spacious, comfortable to type on, all-in-all I really like this thing. (If only they hadn't used Arial for the keycaps!)

Logitech M325

What can I say, it's a… mouse. I basically grew up with one of these (and I really mean one, they're crazy reliable) so when looking for a new mouse, I had no better idea than just buy it again. Big fan of the fast-scrolling wheel! (Miss me with that laggy, stepper nonsense.)

For the lack of a better comparison, this mouse kinda feels like the AK-47 of mice – deliberately simple, super reliable, and you lowkey see them everywhere.

Sony DualSense

Replaced a DualShock 4 (whose left joystick unevitably went haywire), the most prominent improvements being ergonomy (this one actually fits in the hand for a change!) and USB-C (making MicroUSB officially GONE from my everyday life – finally). Though honestly I'm not confident about its joysticks lasting any longer than the DS4's. I've been eyeing the 8BitDo Ultimate lately.

Superlux HD672

This is probably my biggest frugal project to date. For only around 30€, these are paradoxically the closest I've gotten so far to “real”, nice-sounding headphones.

So Superlux is apparently this Taiwanese company, best known for AKG clones and the like. But they also have some interesting original models in their lineup. After hearing some nice things about this brand (mostly on the awesome DYI Audio Heaven blog), the HD672 finally concluded my search for over-ear headphones. There's one weirdness-slash-awesomeness: the earpads, for some inexplicable reason, are made of silicone. Not your usual faux leather or textile, just straight-up rubber. It's only slightly less sweaty than it sounds, but it works suprisingly well!

iPad Air 3 (2019)

Yeah it's an older one. I don't really love iPadOS as a platform, for the record, so that's part of the reason. But what are you gonna do, not have an iPad??
